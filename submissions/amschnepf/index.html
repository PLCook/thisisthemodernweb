GOAL: 50 BOOKS!

January

Dash and Lily's Book of Dares

by Rachel Cohen and David Levithan
Lily decides to leave a little red notebook full of dares on a shelf in her favorite bookstore. She knows only the perfect boy will be up for the challenge. This is where Dash comes in. Full of dares, clues, and friendship; Book of Dares is a great YA fiction about adventure and budding romance.
My Rating: ➊ ➋ ➌ ➃ ➄

Eat, Pray, Love

by Elizabeth Gilbert
Eat, Pray, Love is the autobiography of Elizabeth Gilbert. Elizabeth is a 30-something struggling through a mid-life crisis. She decides to leave everything behind, and spend a year soul-searching. Experiencing pleasure in Italy, devotion in India, and enlightenment in Bali, she comes to find that life is what you make it, and you can only survive once you love yourself.
My Rating: ➊ ➋ ➌ ➍ ➎

February

Gone Girl

by Gillian Flynn
It's Nick and Amy's 5th wedding anniversary; Amy goes missing. Is Nick a killer? Is Amy a psychopath? Through a series of diary entries, scavenger hunt clues, and interviews with Amy's "closest" friends, find out what happened between this disfunctional couple.
My Rating: ➊ ➋ ➂ ➃ ➄

March

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone

by JK Rowling
The first book in the Harry Potter (7 book) series, The Sorcerer's Stone follows a young Harry Potter through everything from his home life with the Dursleys to finding out he isn't like most other kids. A magical beginning to the ultimate adventure of friendship, family, and self-discovery.
My Rating: ➊ ➋ ➌ ➍ ➎

Animal Farm

by George Orwell
What happens when the animals on Manor Farm get tired of following human orders? REVOLT! At first Animal Farm is a success, Animalism is a hit, and everyone has a job that helps everyone else. It's only a matter of time before power and control are more appealing than equality. All animals are (not) equal...
My Rating: ➊ ➋ ➌ ➍ ➎

April

Fangirl

by Rainbow Rowell
Fangirl follows Cath Avery to college where she has to decide if she is getting to old for fangirling. she has been writting her own, very popular, fanfic for many years and her readers are expecting an ending. But college comes with new friends, boys, and homework that Cath never anticipated. Will her roommate like her? Will she get the guy? Will she finish her fanfic AND all of her homework in time?
My Rating: ➊ ➋ ➌ ➍ ➄

Divergent

by Veronica Roth
Divergent is the first of three books in the Divergent Series. This dystopian novel set in Chicago where the society is split into 5 factions. When citizens turn 16 the have to make a decision: stay with their families in the faction where they were born, or abandon everything they've ever known for a new faction. Divergent follows Tris through her difficult decision, and a highly competitive initiation process. Tris must also keep a dangerous secret.
My Rating: ➊ ➋ ➌ ➍ ➎

May

Insurgent

by Veronica Roth
Insurgent: The second book in the Divergent Series. Conflict grows, secrets emmerge, sides are chosen. Tris must embrass her secret, and potentially rest everything by doing so.
My Rating: ➊ ➋ ➌ ➃ ➄
