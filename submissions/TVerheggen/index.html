Cahokia Mounds, IL

I got my start in prehistoric archaeology digging a pre-Cahokian site near Southern Illinois University, Edwardsville. My job (once in the lab) was to look for evidence of maize. We were trying to track down the entrance of corn into local society. Cahokia Mounds is about 20 minutes east of St. Louis, MO.
Visit Cahokia!

Olympic Peninsula, WA

I worked along-side the Lower Elwha K'lallam Tribe in recovery of an amazing site on the Puget Sound, Olympic Peninsula, WA.
Read about the Tse-Whit-Zen site & the tribe.

Outer Banks, NC

We watched the dolphins play and historic ships sail by while working near the Outer Banks, NC. The surf isn't all that great in the Outer Banks, and working on a beach is actually pretty awful. As always, the crew (family) keeps it awesome.

Buffalo, WY

I hiked all day on ranch lands near the foothills of the Little Big Horn Mtns in Wyoming. Pretty much awesome. This is by far my favorite country and the sky really is BIG!

All work & no play?

The Mayan Route

I took four or five months off to travel the Mayan Route & see ruins in Central America. After finishing language school in Costa Rica we meandered up to the Yucatan & saw lots of non-Mayan archaeology in Nicaragua & Honduras before arrivinga at the Mayan sites of Copán Ruinas (N. Honduras), Palenque, Chichén Itzá, & Tulum (S. Mexico).
